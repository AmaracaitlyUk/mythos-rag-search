Giants in Mythology

Giants, oversized humanoid beings of immense strength, appear repeatedly
across world mythology, often representing an older, more chaotic era that
came before the current order of gods or humans. In Norse mythology, the
jotnar are a race of giants who predate the gods and remain locked in an
ongoing rivalry with them, sometimes as enemies and sometimes, through
intermarriage, as relatives; the giant Ymir's body is even said to have
formed the world itself after being slain.

Greek mythology similarly features the Titans, an earlier generation of
giant deities overthrown by Zeus and the Olympians, as well as the Gigantes,
who later rebelled against Olympus in the Gigantomachy. Giants also appear
widely outside these two traditions, from the giants of Celtic folklore said
to have built ancient stone monuments, to giant figures in various Native
American and other Indigenous storytelling traditions. Across many
cultures, giants often symbolize primal, untamed natural forces that must be
overcome or contained by a newer, more ordered generation of gods or heroes.
