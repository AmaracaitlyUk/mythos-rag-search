Aztec Mythology

Aztec mythology belonged to the Mexica people who built the empire centered
at Tenochtitlan in what is now Mexico. It describes a universe that had
already been created and destroyed four times before the current age, each
previous world ending in a different catastrophe, with the current, fifth
sun sustained through the ongoing efforts and sacrifices of the gods.

Huitzilopochtli, a god of war and the sun, was the patron deity of the
Mexica and was believed to require nourishment through ritual offerings to
continue his daily battle against darkness. Quetzalcoatl, the feathered
serpent, was a more benevolent figure associated with wind, learning, and
the creation of humanity from the bones of previous ages, and appears across
multiple Mesoamerican cultures beyond just the Aztecs. Aztec mythology was
deeply tied to the calendar and agricultural cycles, with major deities and
rituals timed to the seasons that governed the empire's food supply.
