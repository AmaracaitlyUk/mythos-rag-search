Egyptian Mythology

Ancient Egyptian mythology developed over thousands of years and centered
heavily on death, the afterlife, and the cycle of the sun. The sun god Ra
was believed to sail across the sky each day and travel through the
underworld each night, battling the chaos serpent Apep to rise again at
dawn. This daily journey mirrored Egyptian beliefs about death and rebirth.

Osiris, god of the afterlife, was murdered by his brother Set and
resurrected by his wife Isis, becoming the ruler of the dead and a symbol of
resurrection that shaped Egyptian funerary practices, including mummification.
Their son Horus, often depicted as a falcon or a man with a falcon's head,
avenged his father and became associated with kingship, with each living
pharaoh considered his earthly embodiment. Egyptian religion placed enormous
emphasis on preparing for a successful afterlife, which is why tombs were
filled with goods, spells, and elaborate rituals meant to guide the dead
safely through the underworld.
