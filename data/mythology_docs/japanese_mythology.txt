Japanese Mythology

Japanese mythology is closely tied to Shinto, the indigenous belief system of
Japan centered on kami, spirits or gods that inhabit natural features,
ancestors, and abstract forces. According to the creation myth recorded in
the Kojiki, the siblings Izanagi and Izanami created the Japanese islands and
many of the kami by stirring the primordial ocean with a jeweled spear.

The sun goddess Amaterasu is considered the most important kami and the
mythical ancestor of the Japanese imperial line. One famous myth tells how
she once hid in a cave after being offended by her brother Susanoo, plunging
the world into darkness until the other gods lured her out with a mirror and
celebration outside the cave. Japanese folklore also features yokai,
supernatural creatures and spirits ranging from mischievous to dangerous,
such as the fox spirit kitsune and the water-dwelling kappa, which remain
popular figures in Japanese art and storytelling today.
