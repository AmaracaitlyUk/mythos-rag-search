Native American Folklore

Native American folklore encompasses an enormously diverse set of oral
traditions belonging to hundreds of distinct nations and peoples across North
America, each with its own language, history, and mythological figures,
rather than a single unified mythology. Common threads across many, though
not all, of these traditions include a deep spiritual connection to the
natural world, animals as teachers or ancestral relatives, and creation
stories that often begin with a vast primordial water.

Trickster figures appear across many Native American traditions in different
forms, such as Coyote in many Plains and Southwestern stories or Raven among
Pacific Northwest peoples; these characters are clever, mischievous, and
often responsible for both helping humanity (bringing fire, light, or
knowledge) and causing trouble through their own foolishness or greed. Many
stories serve practical and educational purposes, teaching respect for
nature, explaining the origin of animals' distinguishing features, or
passing down lessons about community and moral behavior to younger
generations.
