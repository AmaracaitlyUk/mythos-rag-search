Mythology and Seasonal Festivals

Many mythological stories are closely tied to seasonal change, and numerous
festivals still celebrated today have roots in myths that originally
explained the changing of the seasons. The Greek myth of Persephone, who is
abducted by Hades and spends part of each year in the underworld, was used
to explain winter: while Persephone is below, her grieving mother Demeter,
goddess of the harvest, allows the earth to grow cold and barren, and
spring returns only when Persephone comes back to the surface.

Similar seasonal explanations appear elsewhere: the Chinese Mid-Autumn
Festival is traditionally connected to the story of the moon goddess
Chang'e, while various European midwinter and midsummer festivals absorbed
earlier pre-Christian mythological beliefs about the sun's strength waxing
and waning throughout the year. These myth-linked festivals often served a
practical social function beyond storytelling, marking important
agricultural moments such as planting and harvest times for communities
whose survival depended closely on the changing seasons.
