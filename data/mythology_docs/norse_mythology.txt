Norse Mythology

Norse mythology comes from the pre-Christian religious beliefs of Scandinavia,
preserved mainly in medieval Icelandic texts like the Poetic Edda and Prose
Edda. It centers on Asgard, home of the Aesir gods, chief among them Odin, the
one-eyed god of wisdom and war, and his son Thor, wielder of the hammer
Mjolnir and protector of both gods and humans.

A defining feature of Norse mythology is its sense of impending doom: the
myths foretell Ragnarok, a final battle in which most of the gods, including
Odin and Thor, are fated to die, after which a new, cleaner world will rise
from the ashes. Trickster figure Loki plays a central and destabilizing role
throughout the mythology, both helping and betraying the gods in turn. Norse
myths reflect a warrior culture's preoccupation with fate, honor, and facing
inevitable destruction with courage rather than fear.
