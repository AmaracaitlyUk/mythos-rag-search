Chinese Mythology

Chinese mythology blends elements of ancient folk religion, Taoism,
Buddhism, and Confucian moral teaching, built up over thousands of years
across a vast and diverse culture. One central creation story tells of
Pangu, a giant who separated heaven and earth from a cosmic egg and whose
body, upon his death, became the mountains, rivers, and other features of
the natural world.

The mythology includes a large pantheon of deities and immortals, such as
the Jade Emperor, who rules over heaven, and the Eight Immortals, a group of
legendary figures each representing a different life condition. Dragons hold
a uniquely positive place in Chinese mythology, unlike in many Western
traditions, symbolizing power, good fortune, and imperial authority rather
than danger or evil. Many Chinese myths also explain natural cycles and
festivals still celebrated today, such as the story of Chang'e, the moon
goddess, which is tied to the Mid-Autumn Festival.
