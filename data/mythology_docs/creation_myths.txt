Creation Myths

Nearly every culture has developed its own explanation for how the world,
and humanity, came into existence, and these creation myths tend to fall
into a few recurring patterns despite arising independently across the
globe. Some traditions describe creation from a primordial chaos or void
that gods gradually organize into order, as in the early chapters of Greek
mythology, where Chaos gives rise to the first gods and the ordered
cosmos.

Other traditions describe creation from an original cosmic being whose body
becomes the world, as in the Chinese myth of Pangu or the Norse myth of the
giant Ymir. Still others describe an emergence myth, in which humanity
climbs or is born up through a series of earlier underground worlds before
reaching the current one, a pattern common in various Indigenous traditions
of the American Southwest. A number of traditions, including the biblical
account and several Polynesian myths, describe creation through the direct
speech or will of a single creator god, separating light from darkness or
land from sea in a deliberate, ordered sequence.
